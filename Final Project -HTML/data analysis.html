Data Analysis: Artist Gender, Spotify Popularity, and Album Rankings

This project explores the relationship between artist gender, Spotify popularity, and album rankings for the years 2003, 2012, and 2020. We performed various statistical tests (ANOVA, t-tests, Pearsonâ€™s correlation, linear regression) and visualizations (scatterplots, bar plots, boxplots) to analyze these relationships and determine if artist gender or Spotify popularity has an impact on album rankings over time.

Data Source

The dataset used in this project is publicly available, containing information on album rankings, artist gender, Spotify popularity, and album release years. The data was cleaned and pre-processed before performing any analysis.

Data Cleaning

Before performing any analysis, the dataset was cleaned by handling missing values and filtering irrelevant columns. Necessary transformations were made to prepare the data for statistical testing and visualizations.

Analysis Approach

The analysis involves the following key steps:

  • Statistical Tests: ANOVA, t-tests, and linear regression were used to test the significance of artist gender, Spotify popularity, and release year on album rankings.
  • Visualizations: Scatterplots, bar plots, and boxplots were used to visually represent the relationships between variables.

Results and Findings

  • The **mean rank** for male albums in 2020 is 242.34, for female albums is 266.41, for male/female albums is 272.68, and for NA is 451.00.
  • The **ANOVA** showed a **p-value of 0.185**, indicating no significant difference in rank based on artist gender.
  • The **t-test** confirmed that there is no significant difference in mean rankings between male and female albums, with a p-value of 0.1576.
  • The **linear regression** model predicted 2020 album rankings based on Spotify popularity, showing a **significant negative correlation** (r = -0.2215) with a **p-value < 0.05**, suggesting that higher Spotify popularity is associated with worse rankings in 2020.

Conclusion

While some relationships were observed between Spotify popularity and 2020 rankings, no significant differences in rankings were found based on artist gender. This suggests that while gender may be a factor in album marketing and perception, it does not significantly influence rankings when compared to other factors like Spotify popularity.
